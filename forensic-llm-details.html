ForensicLLM: A Local Large Language Model for Digital Forensics

Abstract

Large Language Models (LLMs) excel in diverse natural language tasks but often lack the specialization required for fields such as digital forensics. Moreover, their reliance on cloud-based APIs or high-performance computers restricts use in resource-limited environments, and response hallucinations could compromise their applicability in forensic contexts where adherence to legal standards for evidence admissibility is crucial. To overcome these limitations, we introduce ForensicLLM, a primary effort utilizing a 4-bit quantized LLaMA-3-8B model. ForensicLLM was fine-tuned using Retrieval Augmented Fine-tuning (RAFT) on 6739 Q&A samples extracted with GPT-4 Turbo from a corpus of 1082 digital forensic research articles and meticulously curated digital forensic artifacts. RAFT enables the model to more reliably and accurately leverage trusted digital forensics sources to answer relevant questions for assisting in digital investigations and cite the source of that information. In inference, ForensicLLM uses Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) to access its knowledge base, which can easily be updated without re-training. A comprehensive evaluation was performed on 2246 Q&A samples using three performance metrics that measure the similarity, coherence, consistency, fluency, and relevance of the modelâ€™s responses against the expected responses, along with its reliability at accurately citing sources.We report that ForensicLLM outperformed the base LLaMA-3-8B model by 6.9% on BERTScore F1, 3.2% on BGE-M3 cosine similarity, and 11.3% on GEval. It also achieved improvements of 4.3% on BERTScore F1, 3.1% on BGE-M3 cosine similarity, and 5.7% on G-Eval over base LLaMA- 3-8B+RAG. Additionally, ForensicLLM accurately attributes the sources of its information 84.6% of the time, with 59.0% of these including both the authors and the title. These advancements mark ForensicLLM as a transformative tool in digital forensics, substantially elevating model performance and source attribution in critical investigative contexts.
