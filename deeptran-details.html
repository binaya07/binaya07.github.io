DeepTran: Residual Transformers for Vehicle Energy Consumption Prediction under Real-World Driving Conditions.

Abstract

The global issue of air pollution has raised significant concerns about understanding and predicting vehicle energy consumption. Beyond just vehicle speed, factors like road topology, traffic conditions and driving behaviors play crucial roles in energy consumption. To address this challenge, we introduce a novel framework called DeepTran. Our model leverages deep learning techniques to forecast energy consumption accurately for different vehicle types across a city’s road network, taking real-time traffic conditions into account. DeepTran’s hybrid model integrates a Residual Neural Network (ResNet) into the Vision Transformer architecture to simultaneously capture spatial patterns and extract the temporal dependencies. We incorporate static vehicle data analysis such as vehicle type, weight, engine configuration, and displacement. These elements work in concert to improve the forecasting of spatially correlated time series data. Extensive experiments were conducted on a diverse fleet, including gasoline vehicles, Hybrid Electric Vehicles (HEVs), and Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles/Electric Vehicles (PHEVs/EVs) driving on Michigan’s road network. The results demonstrate that DeepTran outperforms existing prediction algorithms significantly. To promote transparency and reproducibility, the code, dataset, and experimental details are provided on GitHub at https://github.com/binaya07/energy_consumption.
